Introduction

Hydroelectricity harnesses the power of moving water to generate electricity. This project demonstrates how a dam, turbine, and generator work together to produce clean energy.

How It Works

Step 1: Water Flow

Water stored in a dam flows through turbines under high pressure.

Step 2: Turbine Movement

The flowing water spins the turbines, converting water's kinetic energy into mechanical energy.

Step 3: Energy Conversion

The generator connected to the turbines transforms mechanical energy into electrical energy.

Benefits of Hydroelectricity

  • Renewable: Hydroelectricity uses water, an abundant natural resource.
  • Eco-friendly: Produces no greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Reliable: A consistent and controllable energy source.
  • Cost-effective: Low operational costs after initial setup.
